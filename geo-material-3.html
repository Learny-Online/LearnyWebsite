Earth's Structure & Tectonics

The ground beneath our feet is a dynamic system composed of distinct concentric layers constantly interacting with each other.

1. Layers of the Earth

Crust: The brittle, outermost layer where we live. It is the thinnest layer.

Mantle: The thickest layer of hot, semi-solid rock beneath the crust that flows slowly over millions of years.

Core: The center of the Earth, divided into a liquid outer core and a solid iron-nickel inner core.

2. Plate Tectonics & Activity

Pangaea: An ancient, massive supercontinent that existed millions of years ago before breaking apart into modern continents.

Earthquakes: Sudden shakes caused by stress release along tectonic plate boundaries, measured using the Richter scale.

🌋 Lava vs. Magma: Liquid rock is called Magma while trapped beneath the surface. The moment it breaks through and flows out onto the crust, it is called Lava.
