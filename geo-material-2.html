Continents & Oceans

Our planet's surface is split into massive landmasses and enormous bodies of saltwater that dictate ecosystems and weather.

1. The 7 Continents

Asia: The largest continent by both physical land size and total population (housing over 60% of humanity).

Australia: The smallest continent, fully surrounded by water, often called an island continent.

Antarctica: The coldest, driest, and flattest desert on Earth, completely covered by a massive layer of ice.

South America: Home to the world's largest tropical rainforest and river system: the Amazon.

2. The 5 Oceans

Pacific Ocean: The largest and deepest body of water, containing the deepest spot on Earth: the Mariana Trench.

Atlantic Ocean: The second-largest ocean, sitting between Europe/Africa and the Americas.

Arctic Ocean: The smallest, shallowest, and coldest ocean located at the top of the globe.
