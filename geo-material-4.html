Atmosphere & Climate

The atmosphere is a multi-layered shield of gases protecting the planet and creating our shifting weather conditions.

1. Atmospheric Gasses & Layers

Gas Composition: Our air consists primarily of Nitrogen (78%) and Oxygen (21%), along with trace amounts of Carbon Dioxide (CO2).

Troposphere: The lowest layer closest to the ground where all human life, clouds, and daily weather take place.

Stratosphere: The second layer, which directly contains the protective Ozone Layer that filters harmful UV rays.

2. Weather vs. Climate

Weather: The short-term, day-to-day conditions of the atmosphere (e.g., rain today, sunny tomorrow).

Climate: The long-term average pattern of weather in an area calculated over a long period (typically 30 years).

Major Climates: Tropical (hot and humid at the equator), Arid (extremely dry desert zones), and Polar (permanently freezing at the poles).
