Maps & Coordinates

To read a map properly and accurately navigate the world, you must understand how lines of location and structural scales work.

1. Latitude & Longitude

Equator: The primary horizontal reference line located exactly at 0° latitude. It divides the Earth into the Northern and Southern Hemispheres.

Prime Meridian: The primary vertical reference line located at 0° longitude, passing through Greenwich, London. It separates the Eastern and Western Hemispheres.

💡 Quick Pointer: Latitude lines (parallels) run horizontally but measure distances North/South. Longitude lines (meridians) run vertically but measure East/West.

2. Essential Map Components

Legend: A crucial key or guide on a map that explains what each symbol, color, or dashed line represents.

Scale: Represents the ratio of a distance on the map to the actual distance on the ground (e.g., 1:50,000 means 1 cm on the map equals 50,000 cm in reality).

Compass: A directional tool displaying the cardinal points: North, South, East, and West.
